What are you longing to tend right now?
A place. A practice. A relationship. A body rhythm. A garden. A meal. A part of yourself you keep postponing.

To your body. To the earth. To your home. To your work. To your neighbors. To your own pace.

V
Coming Back

The door is always open.

You don't have to have done everything right between the new moon and the full moon. The practice is the returning. Every single time.

The garden does not judge you for the days you don't tend it. It just keeps growing toward the light with whatever it has. That is the thing about tending. It doesn't require perfection. It requires return.

Between now and the Blue Moon on May 31st, you are not being asked to transform. You are being asked to keep coming back. To the longing. To the nourishment. To the rhythm that is yours. To the small flame you named in the lantern tonight.

Some days that will look like bare feet on the ground. Some days it will look like a meal you actually made instead of grabbed. Some days it will just look like closing the digital tools an hour earlier and sitting outside even if it's still cold. Taurus doesn't need it to be beautiful yet. Taurus just needs you to show up.

🌿 Between now and May 31st You don't have to tend the intention perfectly. You just have to know it's held. The lantern is sealed. The return is already written into the cycle. Your only job is to stay curious about what's growing.

Taurus carries things steadily. It doesn't require dramatic action or transformation on a deadline. It asks for one honest beginning — and then the willingness to show up, day by day, to what you've started.

When the Blue Moon opens the lantern on May 31st, you'll read what you wrote tonight. And you'll feel the distance traveled. That distance — that's the work. That's what Taurus asks of us.

🕯️ The Return

The return itself is the change. Not the arrival. Not the staying. The returning. Every time you find your way back to what you planted tonight, you are keeping the flame lit. That's all this asks of you.
